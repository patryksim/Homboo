DESIGN GUIDE LINES

1) Selecting the Right Lot

Keep in mind that most inexpensive lot may not always be the most affordable one, as costs will soar if your builders have extensive pre-building projects to undergo. Things such as clearing trees, compensating for big slops, blasting through rock or providing for extensive drainage are all factors to consider when choosing a lot and locating your new home.

The cost of installing public services is one important factor to consider. The most economica building lots are oftn located in developments with no acces to electricity, gas, and public water lines. So whether you're building a home in a suburban development or on a site with sweeping ocean views, you will almost always need to choose the land before you start designing your floor plans. Factors such as soil condition, drainage, zoning and building codes must also be taken into consideration.

Having an idea of the kind house and style you are planning to build prior to selecting its location is very import. the type of house you have in mind generally marks the type of site that makes the most sense to you. Do you want a house an a cliff over-viewing the sea? How about a house on a golf course? Or do you want your house to be designed as a single-level or two-level home? Thesee are important issues to be answered before selecting a lot, as these fact will affect the location, size and dimensioning of your site.

1-1) How To, "Tips": Selecting the right lot and creating the right style, floor plan foy you

The home style wished and location goes hand in hand when selecting the lot/land of your new home. We understand the options may seem endless when considering all that will go into your vision of your finished home. For example, if your heart is set on a Modern/Contemporary-style home, a heavily-treed lot may not make sense for you.

Having an idea of the architectural style you prefer before purchasing a lot will determine the size and characteristics of your buildinf site. you may run into problem if you design a specific floor plan too soon. In order to avoid landscape alterations during the building process, we recommend designing the floor plan of your desired architectural style to suit a certain landscape, as you may not want to alter a landscape to accommodate the specifications of you house plans. The configuration of rooms, the placement of the driveway, as well as many other desing elements will be affected by the land you choose... A well planned architecture desing will take full advantage of your lot's peculiarities and context.

Therefore, it is always recommended to spend time on the building site. Walk the full length of your lot at diferent times of the day. If you are concerned about your footprint on Earth, you may want to think about the land in terms of correct base to take full advantage and generate a powerful architectural statement among other things.

There other aspect Homboo suggests taking into consideration when designing your new house:

  • What are the general characteristics of the land? Consider its Surroundings and context to neigbor properties in the area.
  • Are othe structures clearly seen from the building lot? What is the prevailing architectural style? Will your dream home fit the overall context of the neighborhood?
  • Will the size of your house be proportionate to the size of the lot?
  • Is the lot/land located on a street or road? Should the house face toward or away from the road? Where should the driveway be located?
  • Take into consideration the views, as well as the location, of the sunrise and sunset all directions. You can tailor the views you would you like to see from all the living areas if planned properly. That is a huge benefit of custom home-building.
  • Consider the relevance of cooling and heating costs.
  • Terrain plays a huge role in whether the style you desire in a home is appropiate for the surrounding other geological conditions that might affect the desing or placement of your home.
  • The preparation of the land for building, planting trees will also add to your final costs of not considered in your budget, it seems like a lot to absorb, but as you narrow your search for an ideal site, remember the importance of seeking and receiving our expert's advice on home building. Our experts, support team are here to help and will happily offer the expert guidance your project demands. Not to mention our architects will investigate and determine the characteristics of the land, explore zoning, building codes and other factors.

Land Conditions

  • Soil. Has the property been a vitim of hazardous waste? Are there pollutants that may not be apparent to an untrained observer?
  • Land Stability. Is the lot of property you are interested in subject to land slides or other land sink holes?
  • Water Drainage. Consider the possibility of all downsides of any location before choosing. For example, is the lot you desire located near the sea or a river? is it locat located on a low spot which may make your home subject to water runoff? Err on the side of caution.
  • Noise. Is there a nearby airport, highway, or railroad? How noisy is it?

1-2) Site Preparation

Preparing a site for construction can have a big impact on the cost a home. Building on a flat lot will usually cost less. If you have to haul in lots of dirt, do a lot of grading, clear trees, or blast throungh large rocks, then site preparations can become more expensive.

1-3) Zoning, Building Codes and More

  • Zoning. While the lot may be beautifully-placed, consider the possibility that in five years, your beautiful views may be replaced by a highway or a housing development. Zoning regulations will indicate what may be legally constructed in the surrounding area.
  • Building Codes. A variety of ordinances will affect th placement of your new home on the lot. Regulations will specify how clase you can build to the property line, roads, streams, and lakes.
  • Easements. Easements for electricial and telephone poles will limit the space you have for building your home.
  • Public Utilities. Unless the property is in a development of suburban tract homes, there may not be easy access to electricity, gas, telephone, cable telecision, public water lines or sewers. If there are no municipal sewers, you'll need know where you may legally place your septic system.

Costs

Homboo's experts are keen on budgeting processes. While you may be tempted to cut the costs of your land in order to spend more money on the building of your house, this is not recommended. Allow us to explain imperative things such as the cost od altering an unsuitable lot and how it is likely to be more expensive than purchasing land that meets your needs and your dreams. As experienced field experts, we will recommend smart ways to spend properly on the lot of land for your new home. While there are exeptions, in most communities your land will represent 20% to 25% of your total building costs. Small details in the design of your home can make a big difference in the price. To save on costs, begin estimating construction expenses before you design your final blueprints
