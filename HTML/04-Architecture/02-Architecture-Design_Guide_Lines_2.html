2) DESIGNING YOUR HOME: IMPORTANT FACTORS TO CONSIDER WHEN DESIGNING YOUR NEW HOME

STARTING THE DESIGN PROCESS


Define How You Live

One of the most important aspects in designing your house is how you want to live and how to translate and integrate that information into your custom design.

In order to design your perfect dream house, you will have to clearly understand and define your lifestyle with your design team. For example: You must consider where exactly you want to have the main meeting area in your house? Which room will be the focal point? What are the activities you want to have in this and other areas? Do you need a home office? A guest room? Will they be the same one? Do you want a front porch, a big terrace or a patio? How do you want these spaces to interact among one another? Are you a fan of open space? Or do you want to delineate each room with its own character?

These are just few aspects of what you have to understand in order to design your perfect dream house. This is one service Homboo will help you understand and execute.

The Size of Your Future Home Matters

When building a home, it's best to work with even numbers. It is always better to round to increments of two feet. This reduces work and wasted materials. Also, it's most economical to build a home which is no deeper than 32 feet.If the depth exceeds 32 feet, if so, your roof trusses will need to be custom made and this may add more extra costs to your project.

The Shape

Always assume that homes with a rectangular or box shape must cost less to build. This idea is derived from the concept that adding more angles and corners in your home could increase the amount of materials and the amount of labor needed to build your home.

However, our architects are well-trained experts and they can help achieve much more exciting designs without getting you out of budget. Our ability to provide the results you seek without exceeding your carefully-planned budges is one of Homboo's missions.

Organize Your Ideas around a Solid Concept

A well-achieved design always derives from a well-planned concept. Good concepts serve as idea organizer and help us keep our thoughts together in one direction. The design concept of your house can be simple or elaborate, however, what is most important to know is that we must understand your priorities and needs prior to designing your home.

It's a like a frame that holds together in harmony, the relation between the spaces within and around.

Consider the conforming elements of a design concept and how it can be obtained. A design concept articulates the interaction among the different aspects involved through the design process.

These aspects are: shape / form, architectural style, function / life style, space organizing concept, sustainability, etc. Consider how it is created. This can be done by simply defining and understanding each one of the elements above mentioned.

Next, you will find a brief dictionary of terms:

Form: The form or shape of your home is the direct result of distributing the different spaces required to satisfy your specific needs.

Architectural Style: The style used for your house is determined by the architecture language used in terms of form, techniques, materials, time period, region and other factors such as cultural context, etc. Therefore, a style is a way of expressing the language used to define and emphasize the expression and looks of a design.

Symmetry in the Design: Symmetry is a designing tool that helps create balance and harmony to the design. Symmetry has played an important role in important historical buildings; however, it may not be a very good resource to create an exciting design. Good compositions can be achieved creating well balanced forms.

Balance in the Design: As we mention above, balance is an important designing tool to help create visual weight, as well as a feeling of stability to the overal design of your house. It can be achieved by drawing an imaginary centerline through the center of a specific facade or floor plan and creating a proportional equality in weight to both sides of that imaginary line. Balance can be symmetrical or asymmetrical as long as the house expresses and maintains a sense of equilibrium.

Scale: Proportion is usually defined as the relationship between two things of different size. In architecture, proportion is usually used to describe the relationship between the size of spaces and masses in relation to the human body's size. This kind of proportion is called scale. Scale is a very important tool use to greatly affect the way a person perceives and relates to a space. Sometimes designers and architects intentionally design big spaces in order to emphasize specific things or a definite architectural idea. For example, a very tall space makes people feel quite small in comparison to the remarkable power of God. Architects also deliberately design spaces with different scales in order to create a dynamic movement through these spaces.

Function & Lifestyle: These two terms can be derive from observing historical and today people's life patterns from different areas, and regions of the world By looking at how we go about our day-to-day activities, we can easily identify the specific patterns followed. These are the so-called social / personal / family-established patterns that reflect our own particular lifestyles. Sustainability: It is the capacity to endure. Sustainable architecture seeks to minimize the negative environmental impact of buildings by enhancing efficiency and moderation in the use of materials, energy, and development space. It ensures that our actions and decisions today do not inhibit the opportunities of future generations. This term can also be used to describe an energy and ecologically conscious approach to the design of the built environment.

Create the Right Layout for You

The objective at this stage is to develop the layout that will integrate best your lifestyle and lot's characteristics (geometry, topography, views, etc.).

In creating this layout, it is critical to first define the way you will organize all the different spaces around the house, taking into consideration the above mentioned elements. This organizing process can be easily achieved by drawing the ideal Flow Axis for a particular house project.

This organizing tool helps us illustrate, in a graphic manner, the circulation flow as we go from one space to the other taking into consideration individual lifestyle and lot characteristics. Some of these circulation flow axes are: linear -- or I, cross +, T, Round, etc. (Include here schematic floor distribution diagrams)

Using the flow axis concept will help you create the flow that combines in perfect harmony your home and life styles with your lot's specific characteristics. This in turn will make your house feel right

Design the Right Space around Your Layout

Homboo recognizes that you just don't want to simply design a house. Rather you want to designed spaces that interact perfectly among them with your home-style.. spaces that fit perfectly your unique needs.

When designing spaces we suggest being rational, simple, sequential and orderly. The objective of a design process is to create spaces that will work well for the people who will be living in their houses. On the other hand, simple shaped spaces work great and are very efficient. Don't just go into complex shapes just for the sake of it. The right design concept, one that integrates all your needs and etc., must be complemented by each space's shape.

Remember, when designing correct spaces you must take into consideration planned and unplanned human interaction. As an example, a place for planned interaction might include the arrangement of a two or three person sofa, a center table and etc.
The unplanned human interaction might include path, access areas to terraces and etc. Complex shapes are an architectural tool that enables our designers to give accent or to help reinforce certain aspects of a specific design. However, complex shapes (such as triangles, cathedral ceilings, complicated roof lines, etc.) can be difficult and expensive to build.
To save costs, it is suggested to choose square or rectangular floor plans. Avoid cathedral ceilings and complicated roof-lines. Or even better, forget the box and opt for an ultra-affordable dome home. Modern designs provide simplicity, effectiveness and big savings.
Important Elements of Design

What sets apart a well-designed custom house from an impersonal magazine design, is what is always taken into consideration.. the in-between of spaces. Things such as the light/ventilation/location of spaces with respect to views and sun path (trajectory) play a large role in the overall positive representation of your design.

Unity in Variety

A work of architecture has unity when its elements are used and grouped together in a logical manner. Out architects use a variety of elements to add interest and excitement to a design. The main concern is to tie together these various elements in order to give unity to the overall image, as well as creates a sense of harmony. Repetition of shapes and forms unifies the different parts of the building, while the differences between the vertical and curved shapes give the building a balancing sense of variety.

Walking Flow

Well-designed houses should always offer perfect flow among all areas and rooms. There are two types of flow:

1) The one within a space or a room and;

2) The one between two or more spaces or rooms.

The way you naturally flow from one room to the other is most of the time determined by the house-style used in the design. The most compelling spaces are often very dynamic and this is achieved by being part of a larger series of spaces integration. This is form by creating spaces that interact and flow into one another inviting you to explore and experience this set of spaces.

The objective

To create a series of spaces that are able to reveal themselves gradually in an experience that contains both order and mystery. While it is important to have static spaces where one feels perfectly comfortable to remain still, it is also important to have places where one is invited to move further and experience space richness.

The Solution

When planning your movement through space, orchestrate it so each space seems to prepare one space for the next one. The movement through space can be rapidly-flowing to a visible outcome, or many stepped with detours and events along the way. Rooms need not be single units unto themselves. Instead, spaces can be layered such as an atrium, loft, or other enclosures within a larger space.

When we are considering the flow through spaces, we begin by examining the experience of arriving on the site. Then, proceed on to the experience of entering and finally moving through the house.
