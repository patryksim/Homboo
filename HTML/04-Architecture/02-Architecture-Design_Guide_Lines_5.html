WALL FRAMING BASICS

1) Building the Walls

2) Choosing the right material (Lumber, light gage galvanized steel, concrete, etc.),

3) Applying Exterior Sheathing


1) Building the Walls
It is always a must to be aware of proper wall framing basics. This is because the walls of your house (most of the time) will bear the load from the roof down to the foundation and may work as the protector against the elements of changing weather. On the other hand, walls framing must be accomplished with future projects in mind such as kitchen cabinet installation and etc. So, if walls are not constructed properly to its needs they will make these projects difficult to achieve.

The framing basics of your wall include the sill plate on the bottom, the wall studs (which are the vertical beams), and the top sill plate (which is the beam running across the top). It sounds simple in theory, but to build well wall plumbed and squared takes lots of expertise. A recommended method for building a plumb and square wall is to measure from corner to corner. Envision a basic rectangle and then measure from opposite corner to opposite corner (diagonally) and the two diagonals should be exactly the same. (Add Wall Framing Pictures)

Framing a wall, or anything else in a house, seems like a simple concept. If you stand outside your house, as it is under construction, it looks like the builders simply nailed together a bunch of 2x4's in a conceived grid pattern to form a skeleton for the home. There's a lot more to it and especially to do it right. It requires precise measuring, measuring again and then cutting. The old adage of "measure twice, but only cut once" cannot be overstated!

Window and door Openings
Framed walls including openings for windows and doors must include double studs at openings. Wall corners and intersections are built with special stud treatment. Also, let-in brace (diagonal pieces) should be included for rigidity according to the structural design. Studs are notched to receive the brace so the faces of studs are even with the face of brace.

2) Choosing the right material (Lumber, light gage galvanized steel, concrete, etc.),

Termites a Potential Problem?

For the majority of homes constructed in the U.S. (and many other countries), the framing will be done with dimensional lumber. Occasionally, metal framing will be used in place of wood. Metal framing may be a necessity in areas of high termite populations. Homboo's experts can help confirm if your metal framing needs are special order items--in most places they will be--which could add some cost to your house.

Using materials for a house that fend off termites requires different structural design, calculations, coding, etc. Therefore, we suggest discussing these issues also with your future neighbors and with our professionals. This will help give you a picture of what you may possibly encounter in the future.

3) Applying Exterior Sheathing

Sheathing The Walls
Wall framing basics also includes the type of structural wall sheathing you will use to strengthen the walls, add substance to the insulation that you will be using, and provide a base for applying the house exterior and interior siding materials.

Standard exterior structural sheathing is usually made of plywood, oriented strand board (OSB), exterior gypsum board, or wafer-board. If you are considering another type, be sure it is rated as structural wall sheathing. Some builders put sheathing on a framed wall as it lays horizontal on the floor, while others apply it after the wall is tipped up into place. Everybody has their favorite method, largely based on who they learned it from in the first place. Your framing subcontractor will probably have a preference.

Don't Forget the House Wrap

It basically serves as a kind of wind jacket for the house, hinders air from leaking in directly through the walls (mostly), and is also waterproof. Be sure to have more than one person on hand during wall construction because it often requires one or two people to hold things in place while another performs the attaching by nailing everything down.
